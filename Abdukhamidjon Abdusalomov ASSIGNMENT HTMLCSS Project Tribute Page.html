Here is important events' of Elon Musk's life:

  • 1914 - Born in Pretoria, South Africa.

  • 1989 - Musk entered Queen's University in Canada, avoiding mandatory service in the South African military.

  • 1992 - He left Queen's University for studying economics and physics at the University of Pennsylvania.

  • 1994 - Musk held two internships in Silicon Valley at an energy storage start-up called Pinnacle Research Institute, which researched electrolytic ultracapacitors for energy storage, and at the Palo Alto-based start-up Rocket Science Games.

  • 1995 - Musk and his brother Kimbal along with Greg Kouri started Zip2, a web software company, with money raised from a small group of angel investors.

  • 1999 - Musk co-founded X.com, an online financial services and e-mail payment company, with US$10 million from the sale of Zip2. One year later, it was called PayPal.

  • 2002 - PayPal was acquired by eBay for US$1.5 billion in stock, of which Musk received US$165 million. Before its sale, Musk was the company's largest shareholder, owned 11.7% of PayPal's shares.

  • 2002 - Musk founded SpaceX with the long-term goal of creating a true spacefaring civilization. With US$100 million of his early fortune, Space Exploration Technologies Corp., traded as SpaceX.

  • 2006 - NASA announced that SpaceX was one of two selected to provide crew and cargo resupply demonstration contracts to the International Space Station (ISS).

  • 2006 - Musk provided the initial concept and financial capital for SolarCity(develops and sells solar panels and solar roof tiles), which was then co-founded by his cousins Lyndon and Peter Rive.

  • 2008 - Musk assumed leadership of Tesla(American electric vehicle and clean energy company) as CEO and product architect after its true founder Martin Eberhard's departure because of financial crisis and a series of escalating conflicts.

  • 2012 - the SpaceX Dragon vehicle berthed with the ISS, making history as the first commercial company to launch and berth a vehicle to the International Space Station.

  • 2013 - Musk unveiled a concept for a high-speed transportation(Hyperloop) system incorporating reduced-pressure tubes in which pressurized capsules ride on an air cushion driven by linear induction motors and air compressors.

  • 2016 - while stuck in traffic, Musk tweeted "[I] am going to build a tunnel boring machine and just start digging ..." The company was named 'The Boring Company' and it builds tunnels. Its current and proposed projects are designed for Loop and Hyperloop-based transportation.

  • 2020 - SpaceX launched its first manned flight called Demo-2 becoming the first private company to both place a person into orbit and to eventually dock a crewed space-craft with the ISS.
